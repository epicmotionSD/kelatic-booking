# KeLatic Organic Transition Plan

## Situation

KeLatic's remaining growth gap is not primarily a budget issue. It is an infrastructure issue.

Google Ads is still carrying demand because the organic discovery layer is not yet strong enough to replace those bookings. If paid search is cut before the organic layer is ready, the business drops appointment volume with no replacement channel to catch it.

For weekly execution, owners, and KPI tracking, use [kelatic-transition-execution-checklist.md](c:/Users/shawn/Downloads/kelatic-booking/docs/kelatic-transition-execution-checklist.md).

## The Sequencing Rule

The order matters:

1. Fix the booking conversion leak
2. Strengthen Google Business Profile and Yelp
3. Shift the existing paid budget into retargeting
4. Pause Google Ads only after organic demand proves it can carry the calendar

## Why Conversion Comes First

Current booking-page-to-appointment conversion is about 15%.

For a strong local service booking page, the expected range is closer to 22-28%.

That means the highest-leverage move is not more traffic. It is capturing more of the traffic already arriving.

Estimated impact:

- Closing the conversion gap at current traffic levels is worth about $2,100/month in recovered revenue
- This is revenue KeLatic can recover before adding new acquisition complexity

## Immediate Same-Day Fixes

These are the fastest changes with the clearest impact:

- Replace the bio link with a direct booking URL
- Remove `Linktree` or any extra routing layer for booking traffic
- Add scarcity indicators on the booking page and key booking CTAs
- Add booking stickers on Instagram Stories
- Use booking-first language in captions, Stories, and profile actions

## Free Traffic Pillar 1: Google Business Profile

Google Business Profile is the primary replacement for the local search demand currently being bought through Google Ads.

Execution standard:

- Use keyword-rich service descriptions
- Add at least 20 strong photos
- Publish weekly posts
- Keep service categories, hours, and service details current
- Focus on ranking for terms such as `locs Houston` and `loc retwist near me`

## Free Traffic Pillar 2: Yelp

Yelp is the second free traffic pillar because local service customers still use it heavily when comparing providers with open availability.

Execution standard:

- Add 10 or more new reviews
- Keep service information and hours accurate
- Highlight availability and responsiveness
- Position KeLatic to capture overflow demand from competitors who are fully booked or have limited operating hours

## Paid Social Reallocation

The current monthly paid budget should not disappear. It should move only after the foundations are in place.

Recommended redeployment:

- Shift the existing spend into paid social retargeting in Week 4
- Prioritize profile visitors, Reel engagers, and site visitors
- Treat this as warm-audience conversion support, not cold traffic acquisition

This should convert better than cold local search once the remarketing audiences are large enough.

## Four-Week Operating Sequence

### Week 1: Patch the Booking Leak

- Keep Google Ads running
- Implement direct booking entry points everywhere
- Add scarcity indicators and booking stickers
- Measure booking-page-to-appointment conversion daily

### Week 2: Build Google Business Profile Strength

- Rewrite service descriptions
- Upload fresh media
- Start weekly GBP posting cadence
- Measure calls, clicks, and direction requests

### Week 3: Build Yelp Capture Capacity

- Request reviews aggressively from happy clients
- Refresh Yelp profile content
- Watch for bookings coming from discovery rather than paid search

### Week 4: Reallocate Carefully

- Move the $280 into paid social retargeting only if the green-light checks are met
- Pause Google Ads instead of deleting it
- Preserve the account, conversion history, pixel data, and audiences as a 30-day safety net

## Six Green-Light Checks

Google Ads should only be paused when all six checks are true:

1. Booking-page-to-appointment conversion is materially improved and tracking toward the 22-28% benchmark
2. Instagram and social traffic goes directly to the booking page with no unnecessary routing steps
3. Google Business Profile has keyword-rich service descriptions, 20+ photos, and weekly posts in place
4. Yelp has at least 10 new reviews and a fully current profile
5. Paid social retargeting audiences and tracking are ready before any budget shift
6. The calendar is 80%+ filled from organic sources for two consecutive weeks

## Safe Cutover Rule

Do not delete the Google Ads account when the time comes.

Use this sequence instead:

1. Pause the account
2. Hold for 30 days
3. Monitor booking volume, organic share, and retargeting performance
4. Reinstate only if organic and retargeting fail to hold the booking volume

## Decision Standard

The business is ready to reduce Google search only when organic discovery is already doing the work.

If the calendar is not 80%+ filled from organic sources for two consecutive weeks, the infrastructure is not ready and the account should stay on.
